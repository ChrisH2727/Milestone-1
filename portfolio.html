What I have been working on recently

Environmental Monitoring

Climate change is happening. CO2 levels have increased from 300ppm in the 1950s to in excess of 400ppm in 2020s. As a concequence, sea levels have risen and the planet is experiencing more more extreme weather patterns. The goal of these projects and those that will follow is to monitor various environmental levels and to report on them.

Air Quality Monitor

Exposure to high levels of air pollution can cause a many adverse health problems. It increases the risk of respiratory infections, heart disease and lung cancer.
My prototype Air Quality monitor. It measures air particulate levels, VOCs, CO2 temperature humidity and pressure. Values are sent via wifi to ThingSpeak on-line IoT analytics platform.

Chemical Ferilizer Run-off

Aproximately 270 million tons of chemical fertiliser was used world wide in 2020. Between 20% and 60% ends up poluting the environment and adding to greenhouse gasses.
My NPK Monitor is designed to measures levels of nitrogen phosphorus and potasium in the soil. With the addition of a GPS receiver (not shown) it should be possible to determine where fertilizer should be applied and where an excess is already present.

My Approach to the Hardware and Software

In all cases I use low cost commercial hardware. In fact the ESP32 micro controller that I use in some of my projects includes two cores, WiFi connectivity, a veriety of interface busses all for less than the price of half a pint of beer!
The embedded software is written in a flavour of "C" and uses available sensor libraries available from the manufacturers. As some of the sensors have timing contraints (need to be put to sleep and woken up at periodic intervals) and because I want sensor data sent to the IoT platform at periodic intervals, I have written and use my own real-time scheduler.

A Final Word

While all sensors provide data which appears to be correct, none have been calibrated. All that I can can realistically claim is that sensor readings increase and decrease relative to changes in their environment against an arbitary calibration.
Nonetheless, my goal is to investigate the use and performance of more sensors monotoring the environment and to make the data available on the internet for others to see and thereby further raise awareness of climate change and its impact.
